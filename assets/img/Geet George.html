About Me

I am Geet George, a post-doctoral researcher working with the Tropical Cloud Observations group at the Max-Planck-Institute for Meteorology. My research mainly circles around the coupling of circulation and clouds in the tropics.


I finished my PhD in 2021 from the Max Planck Institute for Meteorology. For my doctoral research, I described the trade-wind meso-scale environments using measurements mainly from dropsondes, and quantified cloudiness via different measures using different airborne and spaceborne remote-sensing instruments. At the core of describing mesoscale environment was the estimation of kinematic factors such as divergence, vorticity and vertical velocity. This characterisation from colocated measurements made it possible to understand the interplay between clouds, moisture and mesoscale circulations.

Research

I am interested in studying the interaction of tropical clouds with their mesoscale environments, especially in terms of atmospheric dynamics. My primary tools in this investigation are measurements made during field campaigns such as EUREC4A. Recently I have been employing satellite observations to further such investigations.

Current Research

My current fascination is with the ubiquitous presence of Shallow Mesoscale Overturning Circulations (SMOCs) in the northern Atlantic trade-wind regions. They were discovered from measurements made during the EUREC4A field campaign. After working with the in-situ field measurements, I am now currently looking into characterizing their features from satellite observations.
